I am a computer vision and machine learning scientist at Vicon. Before that, I was a researcher at Oxford Centre for Functional MR Imaging of the Brain (FMRIB), University of Oxford, working with Mark Chiew.

I had my undergraduate degree in Electrical Engineering from Amirkabir University, Tehran. After working as an embedded systems developer for a couple of years, I moved to Ankara where I did my graduate work in the lab of Tolga Çukur.

My research centers on developing machine learning tools for motion capture. Also, I am interested in developing methods to make MR imaging faster and more accurate. Recently, I am focused on reducing motion artifacts in functional MRI in awake non-human primates and in rodents. Another wing of my interests is developing computational models to map representation of visual and auditory natural stimuli in the human brain.
